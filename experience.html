Dec 2023 – Nov 2024

At SCORPE Tech, I worked on the development of autonomous drone systems integrating computer vision, embedded processing, and real-time control. My primary focus was on perception pipelines, hardware integration, and autonomous navigation reliability.

I contributed to building a real-time license plate recognition (LPR) system by collecting and labeling over 900 images, managing dataset splits, and training a YOLOv8-based detection model. The trained model achieved high accuracy while maintaining low-latency inference suitable for onboard deployment.

Beyond model training, I worked on integrating the AI perception pipeline into the drone’s flight-control stack, ensuring reliable communication between the vision system, onboard compute, and navigation logic. This required careful consideration of latency, computational limits, and system stability.

I also supported hardware integration tasks, including camera installation and calibration using a gimbal-stabilized setup, autonomous route generation, and LiDAR-based obstacle avoidance. These contributions helped improve flight safety and reduce collision risk in complex environments.
