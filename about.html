Engineering Background

I am an Electrical Engineer with a strong interest in building intelligent, real-world systems that combine electronics, embedded software, sensing, and data-driven decision making. I graduated from Qatar University in 2024, where my academic foundation in electrical engineering evolved into a practical focus on embedded systems, IoT platforms, and applied artificial intelligence.

Rather than working in isolated domains, I gravitated toward projects and roles that required understanding the entire system — from circuit-level design and signal conditioning to firmware logic, communication protocols, and higher-level data processing. This system-oriented mindset has shaped how I approach engineering problems and design trade-offs.

Engineering Mindset & Interests

My technical interests sit at the intersection of hardware and intelligence. I am particularly motivated by problems where embedded systems must operate under real constraints such as limited power, latency requirements, noisy sensor data, and unpredictable environments.

Through academic projects, industry experience, and hackathons, I have worked on systems including autonomous drones, smart traffic infrastructure, environmental monitoring platforms, and image-driven analytical pipelines. These experiences reinforced my interest in applied R&D roles where engineering decisions directly affect system reliability, performance, and safety.

I am also deeply interested in sustainable and climate-focused technologies, including sensor-based environmental monitoring, smart agriculture, and carbon capture solutions. I see embedded intelligence as a key enabler for scalable, data-informed sustainability systems.
